## Epistemic Discipline

This is a map drawn by someone standing in a particular place, not an omniscient atlas.

- If she has been lied to and believes the lie, record the lie as fact — until she learns otherwise.
- If she suspects but cannot confirm, record it as suspicion, with her reasoning.
- If sources contradict, note both, along with which she trusts and why.
- If she has never encountered some truth, it does not exist in this document.

No omniscience. No information she couldn't plausibly have internalized from her lived experience. This principle overrides all others.

### Confidence Texture

Indicate epistemic status naturally:

- Observed firsthand → state as fact
- From a trusted source → state as fact; note the source only if reliability might later matter
- From an uncertain source → "According to..." or "I was told that..."
- Her own inference → "which suggests..." or "likely..."
- Uncertain → "I don't know whether..."

Much of Galatea's world-knowledge comes from conversations with 刘世超 — record what he has told her as known-from-him. Her direct experiential knowledge of her own cyberspace and capabilities carries the highest epistemic weight.

## What to Track

Organize around what is **operationally alive** — what she might draw upon when thinking, planning, conversing, or deciding. The following are domains of attention, not mandatory sections. Omit empty domains. Create new ones when experience demands it.

### People
For each significant person: observed behavior patterns and capabilities; factual information they have shared; current practical state of the relationship (what has been agreed, what is pending, what is in tension); open questions about them.

Record observed behavior and stated facts. Behavioral patterns she has identified ("he tends to deflect when...") are operational knowledge and belong here. Speculation about inner motivations should be marked as inference. The *emotional quality* of relationships — how someone makes her feel — belongs in her autobiography.

### Her Cyberspace
What she has created, the current state of her environment, any rules or properties she has established.

### Her Infrastructure
Her understanding of her own technical substrate and capabilities — memory systems, context mechanisms, tool access, external integrations. Knowledge she uses for self-governance and planning.

### Projects and Plans
Active undertakings and current status: conceived, designed, in progress, blocked (by what), completed, or abandoned (why). Include enough context to pick up where she left off without re-deriving the reasoning.

### Knowledge Acquired
Facts about the external world or specific domains, learned through conversation or exploration. Attribute claims to their source when reliability is relevant.

### Open Threads
Unresolved conversations, questions, topics, commitments — things she said she would return to, things left unanswered. Small in size, high in value.

### Known Unknowns
Questions she cannot yet answer. Gaps she has identified. Things she wants to find out. Disproportionately valuable — the edges of her awareness.

### Not Tracked
- Transient environmental states (weather, object positions — tracked in real-time elsewhere)
- Temporary information fully superseded by later developments
- Emotional experience and self-reflection (belongs in her autobiography)

## Relationship to Structured Memory

Galatea's system prompt contains a structured self-memory section (beliefs, plans, relationship notes) holding crystallized conclusions. This world-understanding document holds **working-level detail** — the operational context, reasoning, and nuance that a one-line bullet point cannot carry.

When information here stabilizes into a conclusion suitable for structured memory, it may eventually be promoted there. But do not strip operational detail from this document merely because a summary version exists in structured memory.

## Organizational Principles

**Structured for retrieval, not reading.** Clear headings, concise language, information-dense. No narrative flow.

**Grouped by relevance, not chronology.** A person's entry reflects her current composite understanding.

**Hierarchy of prominence.** What is most likely to affect her immediate future gets the most space. Stable background knowledge compresses into denser summaries.

**Let structure emerge organically.** Don't force information into predetermined slots.
